Śivajñāna Bodham — original text, transliteration and translations

Study edition with the Sanskrit and Tamil formulations
For each sūtra, this page provides the original text, a readable Latin transliteration and a study translation. The Sanskrit transliteration was normalized with the help of public witnesses; for the Tamil section I stayed close to the base text used here, so a few words may reflect editorial variation.

Sūtra 1

Sanskrit version

Original
स्त्रीपुन्नपुंसकादित्वाज्जगतः कार्यदर्शनात् । अस्ति कर्ता स हृत्वैतत् सृजत्यस्मात् प्रभुर्हरः ॥ १ ॥
Transliteration
Strīpuṃnapuṃsakāditvāj jagataḥ kāryadarśanāt | Asti kartā sa hṛtvaitat sṛjaty asmāt prabhur haraḥ || 1 ||
Translation
Because the world appears in diverse modes—female, male, neuter and so on—and because it is perceived as an effect, there must be a creator. That Lord, Hara, withdraws the world into Himself and again brings it forth.

Tamil version

Original
அவன் அவள் அது எனும் அவை மூவினைமையின் தோற்றிய திதியே ஒடுங்கி மலத்து உளதாம் அந்தம் ஆதி என்மனார் புலவர்.
Transliteration
Avaṉ avaḷ atu eṉum avai mū-vinai-maiyiṉ tōṟṟiya titiyē oṭuṅki malattu uḷatām antam āti eṉ maṉār pulavar.
Translation
That which is called “he,” “she,” and “it”—the world in its many forms—arises, abides and dissolves. Because it is subject to this threefold process and remains latent in impurity, the wise say that it has beginning and end.

Sūtra 2

Sanskrit version

Original
अन्यस्सन्व्याप्तितोऽनन्य: कर्ता कर्मानुसारत: । करोति संसृतिं पुंसां आज्ञया समवेतया ॥ २ ॥
Transliteration
Anyas san vyāptito ’nanyaḥ kartā karmānusārataḥ | Karoti saṃsṛtiṃ puṃsāṃ ājñayā samavetayā || 2 ||
Translation
He is distinct from the world and the souls, yet by pervading all He is not separate from them. As the agent, and in accordance with karma, He causes the souls to pass through saṃsāra by His inseparable command.

Tamil version

Original
அவையே தானேயாய் இருவினையின்போக்கு வரவு புரிய ஆணையின்நீக்கம் இன்றி நிற்கும் அன்றே.
Transliteration
Avaiyē tāṉēyāy iru-vinaiyiṉ pōkku varavu puriya āṇaiyiṉ nīkkam iṉṟi niṟkum aṉṟē.
Translation
He remains as these things and yet as Himself. Never departing from His command, He causes souls to move through coming and going, birth and death, according to the twofold karma.

Sūtra 3

Sanskrit version

Original
नेतितो ममतोद्रेकादक्षोपरतिबोधतः । स्वापे निर्भोगतो बोधे बोद्धृत्वादस्त्यणुस्तनौ ॥ ३ ॥
Transliteration
Netito mamatodrekād akṣoparatibodhataḥ | Svāpe nirbhogato bodhe bodhṛtvād asty aṇus tanau || 3 ||
Translation
From the negation “this is not I,” from the surge of possessiveness, from awareness when the senses are stilled, from the absence of experience in deep sleep, and from the fact that there is a knower in knowing, one infers the existence of an individual, subtle soul abiding in the body.

Tamil version

Original
உளது இலதென்றலின் எனதுட லென்றலின் ஐம்புலன் ஒடுக்கம் அறிதலின், கண்படில்உண்டிவினை யின்மையின், உணர்த்த உணர்தலின் மாயா வியந்திர தனுவினுள் ஆன்மா.
Transliteration
Uḷadu iladeṉṟaliṉ eṉatu-uṭal eṉṟaliṉ aiṁpulaṉ oṭukkam aṟitaliṉ, kaṇpaṭil-uṇṭi-viṉai yiṉmaiyiṉ, uṇartta uṇartaliṉ, māyā viyantira taṉuviṉuḷ āṉmā.
Translation
Because we say “this exists” and “this does not exist”; because we say “my body”; because we know when the five senses withdraw; because in sleep there is no experience of action or enjoyment; and because we know when caused to know—by all this it is established that there is a soul within the body wrought by māyā.

Sūtra 4

Sanskrit version

Original
आत्मान्तःकरणादन्योऽप्यन्वितो मन्त्रिभूपवत् । अवस्थापंचकस्थ: स्यान्मलरुद्धस्वदृक्क्रिय: ॥ ४ ॥
Transliteration
Ātmāntaḥkaraṇād anyo ’py anvito mantribhūpavat | Avasthāpañcakasthaḥ syān malaruddhasvadṛkkriyaḥ || 4 ||
Translation
The soul is other than the inner instrument—mind, intellect, ego and memory—though joined to it like a king to his minister. It passes through the five states of experience, yet its own knowing and acting are obstructed by mala, impurity.

Tamil version

Original
அந்தக் கரணம் அவற்றின் ஒன்றன்று, சந்தித்தது ஆன்மா சகசமலத் துணராது அமைச்சுஅரசு ஏய்ப்பநின்று அஞ்சவத் தைத்தே.
Transliteration
Antak-karaṇam avatriṉ oṉṟaṉṟu, cantittatu āṉmā sakasa-malattu uṇarātu amaiccu-aracu ēyppa-niṉṟu añcavat-taiththē.
Translation
The soul is not any one of the inner faculties—mind, intellect, ego and memory. It is associated with them, yet different from them. Because of the innate impurity, it does not know its own true nature. It stands like a king beside his minister, passing through the five states of experience.

Sūtra 5

Sanskrit version

Original
विदन्त्यक्षाणि पुंसार्थान्न स्वयं सोऽपि शंभुना । तद्विकारी शिवश्चेन्न कान्तोऽयोवत्स तं नयेत् ॥ ५ ॥
Transliteration
Vidanty akṣāṇi puṃsārthān na svayaṃ so ’pi śaṃbhunā | Tad vikārī śivaś cen na kānto ’yovat sa taṃ nayet || 5 ||
Translation
The senses know objects for the sake of the soul, but not by themselves. In the same way, the soul knows through Śambhu. Yet Śiva is not altered by this; just as a magnet moves iron without becoming iron, He guides the soul without being limited by it.

Tamil version

Original
விளம்பிய உள்ளத்து மெய்வாய் கண் மூக்கு அளந்தளந்து அறியா ஆங்கவை போலத்தாம் தம் உணர்வில் தமியருள்காந்தங் கண்ட பசாசத்து அவையே.
Transliteration
Viḷambiya uḷḷattu meyvāy kaṇ mūkku aḷantaḷantu aṟiyā āṅkavai pōlattām tam uṇarvil tamiya-aruḷ-kāntaṅ kaṇṭa pacācattu avaiyē.
Translation
The organs—skin, mouth, eyes, nose and the rest—grasp their objects only in a limited way; they do not know by themselves. In the same way, the soul does not know by its own isolated power. It knows by Śiva’s grace, just as iron moves in the presence of a magnet.

Sūtra 6

Sanskrit version

Original
अदृश्यं चेदसद्भावो दृश्यं चेज्जडिमा भवेत् । शंभोस्तद्व्यतिरेकेण ज्ञेयं रूपं विदुर्बुधा: ॥ ६ ॥
Transliteration
Adṛśyaṃ ced asadbhāvo dṛśyaṃ ced jaḍimā bhavet | Śaṃbhos tadvyatirekeṇa jñeyaṃ rūpaṃ vidur budhāḥ || 6 ||
Translation
If Śiva were wholly unseen, He could be taken as non-existent. If He were simply visible as an object, He would be inert. Therefore the wise understand that the knowable mode of Śambhu lies beyond both extremes.

Tamil version

Original
உணருரு எனின் அசத்து, உணராது எனின் இன்மையின், இருதிறன் அல்லது சிவசத்தாம் என இரண்டு வகையின் இசைக்கும் மன்னுலகே.
Transliteration
Uṇar-uru eṉiṉ acattu, uṇarātu eṉiṉ iṉmaiyiṉ, iru-tiṟaṉ allatu civa-cattām eṉa iraṇṭu vakaiyiṉ icaikkum maṉṉulakē.
Translation
If we say that Śiva is a knowable form like an object, He would become insentient. If we say that He cannot be known at all, He would seem non-existent. Therefore Śiva fits neither extreme: He is Śiva-sat, conscious reality.

Sūtra 7

Sanskrit version

Original
नाचिच्चित्सन्निधौ किन्तु न वित्तस्ते उभे मिथ: । प्रपंचशिवयोर्वेत्ता यस्स आत्मा तयो: पृथक् ॥ ७ ॥
Transliteration
Nācit citsannidhau kintu na vittas te ubhe mithaḥ | Prapañcaśivayor vettā yas sa ātmā tayoḥ pṛthak || 7 ||
Translation
The non-conscious does not know consciousness, and supreme consciousness is not known by the non-conscious as an ordinary object. That which knows both the manifest world and Śiva is the soul, distinct from both.

Tamil version

Original
யாவையும் சூனியம் சத்து எதிர் ஆகலின் சத்தே அறியாது அசத்து இலது அறியாது இருதிறன் அறிவுளது இரண்டலாத் ஆன்மா.
Transliteration
Yāvaiyum cūṉiyam cattu etir ākaliṉ cattē aṟiyātu acattu ilatu aṟiyātu iru-tiṟaṉ aṟivuḷadu iraṇṭalāt āṉmā.
Translation
In the presence of the Real, all things are as void. The Real does not know the unreal as an ordinary object; and the unreal, being non-conscious, knows nothing. That which knows both—the real and the unreal—and is reducible to neither is the soul.

Sūtra 8

Sanskrit version

Original
स्थित्वा सहेन्द्रियव्याधै: त्वां न वेत्सीति बोधित: । मुक्त्वैतान् गुरुणानन्यो धन्य: प्राप्नोति तत्पदम् ॥ ८ ॥
Transliteration
Sthitvā sahendriyavyādhaiḥ tvāṃ na vetsīti bodhitaḥ | Muktvaitān guruṇānanyo dhanyaḥ prāpnoti tatpadam || 8 ||
Translation
While you remain among the “hunters” that are the senses, you do not know yourself. When instructed by the Guru and released from that identification, becoming non-separate from Śiva, you attain the supreme state.

Tamil version

Original
ஐம்புல வேடரின் அயர்ந்தனை வளர்ந்து எனத் தம்முதல் குருவுமாய்த் தவத்தினில் உணர்த்த விட்டு அன்னியம் இன்மையின் அரன்கழல் செலுமே.
Transliteration
Aiṁpula vēṭariṉ ayarntanai vaḷarntu eṉat tammutal kuruvumāyt tavattiṉil uṇartta viṭṭu aṉṉiyam iṉmaiyiṉ arankaḻal celumē.
Translation
The soul, wearied among the hunters that are the five senses, is awakened by the primordial Guru. Through spiritual discipline He instructs it and makes it leave that confusion behind. Then, realizing its non-separation from Śiva in the final state, it reaches the feet of Hara.

Sūtra 9

Sanskrit version

Original
चिद्दृशात्मनि दृष्ट्वेशं त्यक्त्वा वृत्तिमरीचिकाम् । लब्ध्वा शिवपदच्छायां ध्यायेत्पंचाक्षरीं सुधी: ॥ ९ ॥
Transliteration
Ciddṛśātmani dṛṣṭveśaṃ tyaktvā vṛttimarīcikām | Labdhvā śivapadacchāyāṃ dhyāyet pañcākṣarīṃ sudhīḥ || 9 ||
Translation
Seeing the Lord in the field of inner consciousness, abandoning the mirage of mental modifications, and gaining the shade of Śiva’s feet, the wise one should meditate on the Pañcākṣarī, the five-syllabled mantra: Namaḥ Śivāya.

Tamil version

Original
ஊனக்கண் பாசம் உணராப் பதியை ஞானக் கண்ணினில் சிந்தை நாடி உராத்துனைத்தேர்த்து எனப் பாசம் ஒருவத் தண்ணிழலாம் பதி, விதி எண்ணும் அஞ்செழுத்தே.
Transliteration
Ūṉakkaṇ pācam uṇarāp patiyai ñāṉak-kaṇṇiṉil cintai nāṭi urāttunait-tērttu eṉap pācam oruvat taṇṇiḻalām pati, vidi eṇṇum añceḻuttē.
Translation
The fleshly eye perceives only bondage; it does not perceive the Lord. Therefore, with the eye of knowledge, one should seek the Lord within consciousness itself. Abandoning bondage as one abandons a mirage, one should rest in Śiva’s cool shade and contemplate the five-syllabled mantra: Namaḥ Śivāya.

Sūtra 10

Sanskrit version

Original
शिवेनैक्यं गतसिद्धस्तदधीनस्ववृत्तिक: । मलमायाद्यसंस्पृष्टो भवति स्वानुभूतिमान् ॥ १० ॥
Transliteration
Śivenaikyaṃ gatas siddhas tadadhīnasvavṛttikaḥ | Malamāyādyasaṃspṛṣṭo bhavati svānubhūtimān || 10 ||
Translation
One who attains union with Śiva, becoming perfected, comes to live under His sovereignty. He is no longer touched by mala, māyā and the other impurities, and becomes one established in direct realization.

Tamil version

Original
அவனே தானே ஆகிய அந்நெறியின் ஏகனாகி இறைபணி நிற்க மலமாயை தன்னொடு வல்வினை இன்றே.
Transliteration
Avaṉē tāṉē ākiya aṉneṟiyiṉ ēkaṉāki iṟaipaṇi niṟka mala-māyai taṉṉoṭu valviṉai iṉṟē.
Translation
By that path, the soul becomes one with Him while remaining in the Lord’s service. Then mala, māyā and heavy karma no longer bind it.

Sūtra 11

Sanskrit version

Original
दृशोर्दर्शयितेवात्मा तस्य दर्शयिता शिव: । तस्मात्तश्मिन्परां भक्तिं कुर्यादात्मोपकारके ॥ ११ ॥
Transliteration
Dṛśor darśayitevātmā tasya darśayitā śivaḥ | Tasmāt tasmin parāṃ bhaktiṃ kuryād ātmopakārake || 11 ||
Translation
The soul is that which enables the eyes to see; Śiva is that which enables the soul itself to see. Therefore one should cultivate supreme devotion to Him, the true benefactor of the soul.

Tamil version

Original
காணும் கண்ணுக்குக் காட்டும் உளம்போல காண உள்ளத்தைக் கண்டு காட்டலின் அயரா அன்பின் அரன்கழல் செலுமே.
Transliteration
Kāṇum kaṇṇukkuk kāṭṭum uḷampōla kāṇa uḷḷattaik kaṇṭu kāṭṭaliṉ ayarā aṉpiṉ arankaḻal celumē.
Translation
Just as the mind presents objects to the seeing eye, Śiva illumines the very mind that knows. Therefore, with tireless love, the soul should go to the feet of Hara.

Sūtra 12

Sanskrit version

Original
मुक्त्यै प्राप्य सतस्तेषां भजेद्वेषं शिवालयं । एव विद्याच्छिवज्ञानबोधे शैवार्थनिर्णयं ॥ १२ ॥
Transliteration
Muktyai prāpya satas teṣāṃ bhajed veṣaṃ śivālayam | Evaṃ vidyāc chivajñānabodhe śaivārthanirṇayam || 12 ||
Translation
For liberation, one should approach the true saints, honor their marks and forms, and revere the temple of Śiva. Thus is the essential purport of the Śaiva teaching to be understood in the Śivajñānabodha.

Tamil version

Original
செம்மலர் நோன்தாள் சேரல் ஒட்டா அம்மலங் கழிஇ அன்பரொடு மரீஇ மாலற நேயம் மலிந்தவர் வேடமும் ஆலயம் தானும் அரனெனத் தொழுமே.
Transliteration
Cemmalar nōntāḷ cēral oṭṭā am-malaṅ kaḻi-i aṉparoṭu marī-i mālaṟa nēyam malintavar vēṭamum ālayam tāṉum araneṉat toḻumē.
Translation
With the impurity removed that prevented union with the red-lotus-like feet, the soul should associate with devotees. Rich in love and free from delusion, it should worship as Hara Himself both the form of the devotees and the temple of Śiva.
